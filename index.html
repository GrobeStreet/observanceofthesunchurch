Here's what you do

It's almost suspiciously simple.

No app to download. No mantra to learn. No teacher to find. Just you, the sky, and forty minutes of quiet. Here's the whole thing:

1

Show up 20 minutes early

Check the sunrise and sunset times. Pick whichever is closer. Get yourself outside where you can see the horizon — a beach, a rooftop, a hill, even a west-facing window. You don't need a sacred space. You need a clear sightline.

2

Phone away. Not on silent — away.

This is the hard part for about 90 seconds. Then it's the best part. No music, no podcasts, no “just quickly checking.” The doctrine calls restraint sacred. This is where it starts — with your own attention.

3

Watch the Sun cross the horizon

At sunrise, the first edge breaks through and the world shifts from pre-dawn to day. At sunset, the last sliver disappears and something in the air changes. You're choosing to notice. That's the whole religion.

4

Stay 20 minutes after

Don't leave right away. Don't check your phone. Most people say the clarity hits here — in the quiet after, not during. Your mind settles in a way it hasn't all day.

5

Come back tomorrow. Then the next day.

That's the whole architecture. Repetition, not intensity. The practice deepens not because you had a breakthrough — but because you returned.

Cannabis is recognized as an optional sacrament — used intentionally to support stillness and reflective awareness. Never required. If it doesn't sharpen your clarity, the practice is better served by your sobriety. Every major religion has a sacrament. Ours is governed by the same principle as everything else here: restraint.
